1. Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)

MRR is the lifeblood of any SaaS business. It represents the predictable revenue you can expect each month from your subscribers.

Formula

MRR = Number of Customers x Average Revenue Per User

Or sum up all subscription values:

MRR = Sum of (Price Plan x Number of Customers on Plan)
Calculate Your MRR
Benchmark: There is no "good" MRR number - it depends on your market. But healthy MRR growth is 10-20% month-over-month for early-stage companies.

Why It Matters

MRR is the foundation for nearly every other SaaS metric. It is what investors look at first, what determines your valuation, and what helps you predict future revenue.

Common Mistakes

  • Counting one-time fees as recurring revenue
  • Not accounting for discounts or coupons
  • Forgetting to subtract churned MRR

2. Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)

ARR is simply MRR multiplied by 12. It is the standard metric for larger SaaS companies and is what most investors use for valuations.

Formula

ARR = MRR x 12
Calculate Your ARR
Benchmark: Companies at $1M+ ARR are considered to have found product-market fit. $10M+ ARR puts you in the growth stage.

3. Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)

ARPU tells you how much revenue each customer generates on average. It is crucial for understanding pricing effectiveness and customer value.

Formula

ARPU = Total Revenue / Number of Customers
Calculate Your ARPU
Benchmark: Self-serve SaaS: $10-100/month. Mid-market: $500-2000/month. Enterprise: $5000+/month.

How to Improve ARPU

  1. Raise prices (easiest but risky)
  2. Upsell existing customers to higher tiers
  3. Add premium features or add-ons
  4. Focus on higher-value customer segments

4. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

LTV represents the total revenue you can expect from a single customer over their entire relationship with your business.

Formula

LTV = ARPU x Gross Margin / Churn Rate

Or more simply:

LTV = Average Revenue Per User x Customer Lifespan
Calculate Your LTV
Benchmark: A healthy LTV is at least 3x your CAC. For enterprise SaaS, LTV of $10,000+ is common.
Pro Tip: Calculate LTV separately for each customer segment. Enterprise customers often have 5-10x the LTV of self-serve customers.

5. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

CAC tells you how much it costs to acquire a new customer. This includes all sales and marketing expenses.

Formula

CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Costs / Number of New Customers
Calculate Your CAC
Benchmark: Self-serve SaaS: $50-200. Sales-assisted: $500-2000. Enterprise: $5000-20000.

CAC Components

  • Advertising spend (Google, Facebook, LinkedIn)
  • Content marketing and SEO costs
  • Sales team salaries and commissions
  • Marketing team salaries
  • Tools and software for marketing/sales
  • Events and conferences

6. LTV:CAC Ratio

This is the golden ratio of SaaS unit economics. It tells you if your business model is sustainable.

Formula

LTV:CAC Ratio = Customer Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost
Calculate Your LTV:CAC Ratio
Benchmark:
  • Below 3:1 - You are spending too much to acquire customers
  • 3:1 to 5:1 - Healthy and sustainable
  • Above 5:1 - You could be growing faster (spend more on acquisition)

7. Churn Rate

Churn is the percentage of customers who cancel their subscription in a given period. It is the silent killer of SaaS businesses.

Formula

Churn Rate = (Customers Lost / Total Customers at Start) x 100
Calculate Your Churn Rate
Benchmark:
  • Self-serve B2C: 5-7% monthly is acceptable
  • B2B SMB: 2-5% monthly
  • Enterprise: Less than 1% monthly
Warning: High churn is almost impossible to overcome with growth. A 5% monthly churn means you lose 46% of customers per year. Fix churn before scaling acquisition.

8. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

NRR measures revenue from existing customers, including expansion and contraction. It is the best indicator of product-market fit.

Formula

NRR = (Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR x 100
Calculate Your NRR
Benchmark:
  • Below 100%: Your product is losing customers faster than growing them
  • 100-110%: Good, but room for improvement
  • 110-130%: Excellent, typical of successful SaaS
  • Above 130%: Exceptional, indicates strong expansion revenue
Key Insight: Companies with NRR above 120% can grow revenue without acquiring a single new customer. This is why investors love high NRR.

9. MRR Growth Rate

Your month-over-month MRR growth rate shows how quickly your business is expanding.

Formula

MRR Growth Rate = (New MRR - Lost MRR) / Previous MRR x 100
Calculate Your Growth Rate
Benchmark:
  • Early stage (under $1M ARR): 15-20%+ monthly
  • Growth stage ($1M-$10M ARR): 5-10% monthly
  • Scale stage ($10M+ ARR): 2-5% monthly

10. Burn Rate

Burn rate is how much cash your business is losing each month. It determines how long you can survive before needing more funding.

Formula

Burn Rate = Monthly Expenses - Monthly Revenue
Calculate Your Burn Rate
Benchmark: There is no universal "good" burn rate. The key is ensuring your runway (next metric) is sufficient.

How to Reduce Burn

  • Focus on high-ROI marketing channels only
  • Automate customer support and onboarding
  • Negotiate better terms with vendors
  • Consider remote work to reduce office costs

11. Runway

Runway is how many months your business can survive at current burn rate before running out of cash.

Formula

Runway = Cash Balance / Monthly Burn Rate
Calculate Your Runway
Benchmark:
  • Less than 6 months: Critical - raise or cut costs immediately
  • 6-12 months: Caution - start fundraising or improve revenue
  • 12-18 months: Healthy - time to focus on growth
  • 18+ months: Comfortable - can make bold moves
Rule of Thumb: Always start fundraising when you have 12+ months of runway. Fundraising takes 3-6 months on average.

12. Gross Margin

Gross margin shows what percentage of revenue remains after accounting for the direct costs of delivering your service.

Formula

Gross Margin = (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue x 100
Calculate Your Gross Margin
Benchmark: SaaS companies typically have 70-85% gross margins. Below 60% suggests infrastructure or hosting issues.

What Counts as COGS for SaaS?

  • Hosting and cloud infrastructure (AWS, GCP, Azure)
  • Third-party APIs and services
  • Customer support costs
  • Payment processing fees
  • Onboarding and implementation costs

13. Break-Even Point

The break-even point is when your revenue equals your total costs. Beyond this, every dollar is profit.

Formula

Break-Even Customers = Fixed Costs / (ARPU - Variable Cost Per User)
Calculate Your Break-Even Point
Key Insight: Many SaaS businesses never reach break-even before raising funding. But knowing your break-even helps you understand what scale you need for profitability.

14. SaaS Quick Ratio

The Quick Ratio measures the efficiency of your growth by comparing gains (new MRR + expansion) to losses (churn + contraction).

Formula

Quick Ratio = (New MRR + Expansion MRR) / (Lost MRR + Contraction MRR)
Calculate Your Quick Ratio
Benchmark:
  • Below 1: Your business is shrinking
  • 1-2: Growing but inefficient
  • 2-4: Healthy growth
  • Above 4: Excellent - strong product-market fit

15. Rule of 40

The Rule of 40 is a quick health check: your growth rate plus profit margin should equal at least 40%.

Formula

Rule of 40 Score = Revenue Growth Rate % + Profit Margin %
Calculate Your Rule of 40 Score
Benchmark:
  • Above 40: Healthy - investors will be interested
  • Below 40: Either grow faster or improve margins
Example: A company growing 60% with -10% margins scores 50. A company growing 20% with 25% margins also scores 45. Both pass the Rule of 40.

16. Viral Coefficient

The viral coefficient measures how many new users each existing user brings in. A coefficient above 1 means exponential growth.

Formula

Viral Coefficient = Invites Sent x Conversion Rate
Calculate Your Viral Coefficient
Benchmark:
  • Below 1: Linear growth through referrals
  • Above 1: Exponential viral growth (rare for B2B SaaS)
  • 0.3-0.5: Good for B2B SaaS with referral programs

17. Cohort Analysis

Cohort analysis tracks how groups of customers (cohorts) behave over time. It reveals retention patterns and product health.

How It Works

Group customers by signup month, then track what percentage remain active each month after signup.

Analyze Your Cohorts
What to Look For:
  • Flattening curve: Cohorts that stop churning after 3-6 months indicate strong product-market fit
  • Improving cohorts: If newer cohorts retain better than older ones, your product is improving
  • Early drop-off: High churn in month 1 suggests onboarding issues

18. SaaS Valuation

Valuation estimates what your company is worth. For SaaS, it is typically a multiple of ARR.

Formula

Valuation = ARR x Revenue Multiple
Calculate Your Valuation
Revenue Multiples (2026 Estimates):
  • Public SaaS: 5-15x ARR (varies with interest rates)
  • Growth-stage private: 5-10x ARR
  • Early-stage: 10-50x ARR (based on potential, not current revenue)

Factors That Increase Your Multiple

  • High growth rate (above 50% YoY)
  • Strong net revenue retention (above 110%)
  • Large total addressable market
  • Defensible competitive advantage
  • Efficient growth (high Rule of 40 score)

Bringing It All Together

Tracking these 18 metrics gives you a complete picture of your SaaS business health. But do not try to optimize all of them at once.

The Priority Framework

  1. Early Stage (Pre-Product Market Fit): Focus on churn, NRR, and cohort analysis. These tell you if customers love your product.
  2. Growth Stage: Focus on CAC, LTV, and growth rate. These tell you if you can scale efficiently.
  3. Scale Stage: Focus on Rule of 40, gross margin, and valuation. These tell you if you are building a valuable company.
Final Thought: Metrics are only useful if you act on them. Pick one metric that needs improvement, set a target, and run experiments until you hit it. Then move to the next one.

Rule of 40: The Golden Rule of SaaS Valuation Every Founder Must Know

The Rule of 40 is the quick test investors use to evaluate SaaS health. Learn how to calculate it, what score you need, and how to balance growth vs profitability.

If there's one metric that can instantly tell you whether a SaaS company is healthy, it's the Rule of 40. This simple formula has become the gold standard for evaluating software businesses, and for good reason: it captures the fundamental trade-off every SaaS founder faces between growth and profitability.

The Quick Version

The Rule of 40 states that a healthy SaaS company's growth rate + profit margin should equal at least 40%. A company growing 30% with 10% profit margins (30 + 10 = 40) passes. A company growing 50% but losing 20% (50 - 20 = 30) fails.

What Is the Rule of 40?

The Rule of 40 is a heuristic that emerged from the venture capital and private equity communities to quickly assess SaaS company health. The rule acknowledges a fundamental truth about software businesses: high growth and high profitability are trade-offs, but the sum of both should remain above a certain threshold.

The rule is particularly useful because:

  • It's simple: Anyone can calculate it in seconds
  • It's comparable: Works across different company sizes and stages
  • It's predictive: Companies scoring above 40 tend to command higher valuations
  • It's balanced: Rewards both growth and efficiency

The Formula

Rule of 40 = Revenue Growth Rate % + Profit Margin %

If both are positive, add them. If one is negative, subtract.

How to Calculate Each Component

Revenue Growth Rate:

  • Use year-over-year (YoY) revenue growth for established companies
  • Use annualized month-over-month for earlier-stage companies
  • Typically measured on an ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) basis

Profit Margin:

  • Use EBITDA margin for most calculations
  • Some use Free Cash Flow margin for a stricter view
  • Can be negative for high-growth companies (that's okay!)

Example Calculations

Company A (Growth-Focused):

  • ARR Growth Rate: 60%
  • EBITDA Margin: -15% (losing money to fuel growth)
  • Rule of 40 Score: 60 + (-15) = 45 ✓ PASSES

Company B (Profitability-Focused):

  • ARR Growth Rate: 15%
  • EBITDA Margin: 30%
  • Rule of 40 Score: 15 + 30 = 45 ✓ PASSES

Company C (Struggling):

  • ARR Growth Rate: 20%
  • EBITDA Margin: -5%
  • Rule of 40 Score: 20 + (-5) = 15 ✗ FAILS

Why Investors Care About the Rule of 40

The Rule of 40 has become a key valuation driver. Here's why VCs and public market investors obsess over it:

1. It Predicts Valuation Multiples

Companies with Rule of 40 scores above 40 typically trade at 10-20x revenue multiples. Below 40, multiples drop significantly. Each point above 40 can add 0.5-1x to your revenue multiple.

2. It Indicates Capital Efficiency

A high Rule of 40 score suggests you're not burning cash recklessly. Even money-losing companies can score well if their growth justifies the burn. This signals efficient use of investor capital.

3. It Shows Strategic Clarity

Companies that score well have usually made a clear choice: either grow fast and accept losses, or grow slower but profitably. Low scores often indicate strategic confusion—growing slowly while still losing money.

4. It's a Quick Health Check

For investors evaluating dozens of companies, the Rule of 40 is a 30-second filter. Score above 40? Worth a deeper look. Below 30? Probably pass unless there's a compelling turnaround story.

How to Improve Your Rule of 40 Score

There are two levers you can pull: growth and profitability. The key is knowing which to prioritize based on your stage and market.

Lever 1: Accelerate Growth

Expand Into New Markets

Geographic expansion, new verticals, or enterprise segments can unlock growth without proportional cost increases.

Increase Viral/Referral Growth

Product-led growth with built-in virality lowers CAC and accelerates growth without burning cash.

Improve Retention

Higher retention means more revenue from existing customers, improving both growth and margins.

Launch Upsell Features

New features for existing customers drive expansion revenue at lower cost than new customer acquisition.

Lever 2: Improve Profitability

Optimize CAC

Lower customer acquisition costs directly improve margins. Focus on high-converting channels.

Increase Prices

Raising prices by 10% can improve margins by 5-10% with minimal churn for most SaaS products.

Reduce Gross Margin Drag

Cloud costs, support overhead, and hosting can eat margins. Optimize infrastructure spend.

Cut Non-Essential Spend

Audit all expenses. Cut programs that don't directly drive growth or retention.

The Trade-off Decision

Early stage: Prioritize growth. Accept negative margins if growth is 50%+.
Growth stage: Start improving margins while maintaining 30%+ growth.
Mature: Shift to profitability focus. 15-20% growth with 20-25% margins is excellent.

Common Rule of 40 Mistakes

1. Using the Wrong Time Period

Year-over-year growth is standard. Using month-over-month annualized can inflate your score and make comparisons meaningless.

2. Ignoring Revenue Quality

Not all revenue is equal. A company with 50% growth from low-quality, high-churn customers may score well but have underlying problems.

3. Gaming the Metric

Cutting R&D to boost short-term margins improves your Rule of 40 but hurts long-term competitiveness. Investors see through this.

4. Applying It Too Early

For pre-revenue or very early-stage companies, the Rule of 40 isn't meaningful. Wait until you have $1M+ ARR before obsessing over it.

5. Forgetting Context

A Rule of 40 score of 35 in a recession might be excellent. A score of 50 in a booming market might be mediocre. Always consider macro conditions.

Real-World Examples

Companies That Excel at Rule of 40

When Rule of 40 Doesn't Tell the Whole Story

Salesforce (early years): Grew 50%+ with -30% margins. Score of 20-25, but turned out to be a great investment because they built a dominant market position.

Lesson: The Rule of 40 is a starting point, not the final answer. Use it alongside other metrics and qualitative factors.

Key Takeaways

  1. The Rule of 40 is non-negotiable for mature SaaS: If you're above $10M ARR, you need to score 40+ to command premium valuations.
  2. It's a balancing act: You can achieve 40 through high growth OR high profitability. The best companies do both.
  3. Context matters: Early-stage companies get more slack; mature companies are held to stricter standards.
  4. Don't game it: Short-term manipulation hurts long-term value. Build a genuinely healthy business.
  5. Track it quarterly: Your Rule of 40 should be on every board deck and investor update.

⚡ Quick Timestamps

What is Unix Timestamp?

A Unix timestamp (also called epoch time) is the number of seconds since January 1, 1970 UTC. It's a universal way to represent time that's independent of timezones.

Common formats:
• Seconds: 1709500000 (10 digits)
• Milliseconds: 1709500000000 (13 digits)
• Microseconds: 1709500000000000 (16 digits)
